What is it?

In recent years, the bee population has been at a sharp decline. Scientists have named the phenomenon "colony collapse disorder" (CCD). Dramatic events have occurred, ridding the world of its bees: there has been a loss of 30% from beekeepers in the past 10 years, in both the United States and Europe. In addition, 44 percent of beehives collapsed April 2015-16, which is three times the acceptable amount of beehives gone. This phenomenon is of serious concern to many scientists, who fear a tragic ending to this dramatic event.

Why is it happening?

Scientists have worked hard and long to solve the mystery of why the bee homicide is happening, but they have come up with no completely accepted explanation. However, there is no shortage of plausible theories. Some of the most popular theories scientists have some up with consist of global warming and a disease named varroosis.

Letâ€™s first talk about global warming. Theorists say that the warming of the earth has caused bees to lose 200 miles off of their southern native land. The environment has become too hot to host either bees or the bee food.

Another possible idea is varroosis, a disease caused by parasitic varroa mites. Varroa mites, if left untreated, will suck the blood of developing and adult bees, shortening their lifespans. If varroa mites come in contact with developing honeybees they can end up being deformed with missing wings, legs, and many other clinical deformities. These varroa mites can also harbor pathogens which can make bees sickly and weak.

In addition to global warming and varroa mites, new insecticides like neonicotinoids currently promoted by insecticide companies are lethal to bees. These insecticides also have little advantage over past insecticides. There are many factors that could be causing the dying of the bees, and reversing these factors is of the utmost importance.
